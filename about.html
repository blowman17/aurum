The Atelier

Our story · Our craft · Our world

In 1987, in the heart of Accra, a young textile artist named Kofi Ansah Aurum opened a small workshop with a radical vision: to create garments that honored the immense richness of Ghanaian textile heritage while embracing the precision and rigour of European couture technique. What began as a whisper has become a global conversation about what luxury truly means.

Today, Maison Aurum stands at the intersection of tradition and innovation. Our pieces are not merely designed — they are composed, orchestrated like symphonies of fabric and thread. Each garment begins with hand-selected materials: organic cotton from the northern savanna, indigo dyes harvested by the same family for four generations, kente cloth woven on looms that carry the memory of centuries.

We reject the disposable and celebrate the eternal. In an age of fast fashion and fleeting trends, Aurum creates heirlooms — pieces that will be passed down, treasured, and remembered. This is our promise, and our purpose.

01

Heritage

Every piece draws from centuries of Ghanaian textile artistry — kente weaves, adinkra symbolism, and batik dyeing techniques passed through generations.

02

Craftsmanship

Our master artisans spend between 80 and 400 hours on each piece. Every stitch, every bead, every fold is placed with deliberate intention and care.

03

Sustainability

We source locally, pay fairly, and create garments meant to last lifetimes — not seasons. True luxury leaves the world better than it found it.
